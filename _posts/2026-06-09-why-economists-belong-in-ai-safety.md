I'm starting this site for a simple reason: I'm trying to move into AI safety from economics, and I
think the honest way to do that is **in public**. Not a polished portfolio claiming I've arrived, but
a working record of someone learning the field — reading, building, getting things wrong, and
updating. If you're reading this, you're seeing the beginning of that.

So let me state the bet this whole site is organized around.

### The bet

**The economics of AI is part of the core of AI safety — not a side quest.**

When I first looked at AI safety from the outside, it read as a purely technical field: interpretability,
oversight, evaluations, alignment theory. All essential. But the more I work with multi-agent LLM
systems, the more the *economic* structure of the problem jumps out at me:

- **Alignment is partly an incentive-design problem.** Reward misspecification, Goodhart's law, and
  specification gaming are, in economists' language, moral hazard and mechanism design at a new scale.
- **Multi-agent risk is strategic interaction.** Once you have many capable agents transacting,
  competing, and coordinating, you get collusion, miscoordination, and emergent dynamics — the things
  economics has formal tools for.
- **Evaluation is measurement under adversarial pressure.** Knowing when a capability number means
  what it claims is the same skepticism good empirical economics is built on.
- **The deployment story is political economy.** Whether AI augments or concentrates power isn't
  decided by the model alone; it's shaped by institutions and choices, as Acemoglu and Johnson argue.

None of this makes the technical work less important. It says there's a **bridge** — and that people
who can stand on both sides of it are useful.

### What I'm actually unsure about

I'm in an exploration phase, and I want to be honest about that. I don't yet know whether I'll be
most useful in technical AI governance, in policy, or in technical alignment. I don't know how much
of my "economics is central to safety" view will survive contact with the details. Some of it
probably won't. Writing it down is how I'll find out which parts.
