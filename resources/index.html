resources

A curated, opinionated list of the resources I'm tracking as I learn AI safety — with a bias toward the AI × economics corner of the field.

This is the static, browsable version of what I track. For the interactive map — with relevance scores, “under-explored zones,” and filtering — see the Field Compass. Links here are the ones I’d hand to someone entering the field from an economics or quantitative background.

Orientation & foundations

  • Preventing an AI-related catastrophe — 80,000 Hours’ problem profile; the best single orientation to the field.
  • International AI Safety Report 2026 — Bengio et al.; the authoritative global baseline on capabilities, risks, and safety.
  • Concrete Problems in AI Safety — Amodei et al.; the foundational framing of empirical safety problems.
  • Human Compatible — Stuart Russell’s framing of the alignment problem.
  • aisafety.com — comprehensive community meta-index; a good launch page.

AI evaluations & tooling

  • Evaluating Frontier Models for Dangerous Capabilities — Phuong et al. (DeepMind); a reference point for capability evaluations.
  • Inspect AI — UK AISI’s evaluation framework, with 200+ pre-built evals.
  • METR Task Standard — a schema for evaluating autonomous agents.
  • Model-Written Evaluations — Anthropic; self-evaluation primitives.

Alignment & oversight

  • Constitutional AI — Anthropic’s main alignment approach.
  • Weak-to-Strong Generalization — Burns et al. (OpenAI); a foundational scalable-oversight result.
  • Scaling Laws for Scalable Oversight — oversight under capability asymmetry, in scaling-law form.
  • Sleeper Agents — Anthropic; deceptive alignment, empirically.

AI governance & policy

  • GovAI research — premier academic AI-governance center (Oxford).
  • UK AISI research agenda — a government safety-institute’s priorities.
  • CSET — policy-side research with a strong technical-governance bridge.
  • AI Safety Index — independent assessment of frontier labs.

AI × economics (my corner)

  • Homo Silicus — Horton, Filippas & Manning; LLMs as computational subjects in economic experiments.
  • An Economy of AI Agents — Hadfield & Koh; an economist-facing survey of the agentic-AI landscape.
  • GPTs are GPTs — Eloundou et al.; labor-market exposure to LLMs.
  • Power and Progress — Acemoglu & Johnson; the political economy of automation.
  • Anthropic Economic Index — measuring AI’s real economic footprint.
  • Causal Inference with LLMs: A Survey — Ma et al.; where causal inference meets language models.

Multi-agent & agentic systems

  • Multi-Agent Risks from Advanced AI — Hammond et al.; a taxonomy of multi-agent risk.
  • Generative Agents — Park et al. (Stanford); the architectural reference for multi-agent LLM simulation.
  • Strategic Collusion of LLM Agents — empirical evidence of LLM-agent collusion.
  • Anthropic Cookbook — agentic patterns — official building-block patterns.

Programs, fellowships & funding

  • Anthropic Fellows Program — mentored frontier safety research.
  • MATS — mentored AI-safety apprenticeship (Berkeley / London).
  • SPAR — part-time mentored research, broad aperture.
  • BlueDot Impact — structured AI-safety / AGI-strategy courses.
  • Open Philanthropy — Technical AI Safety RFP — funding directions and a useful map of priority areas.
  • Successif — free career coaching for AI-safety transitions.
